Saydnaya: Inside a Syrian Torture Prison

Forensic Architecture, in collaboration with Amnesty International — 2016
01 Datasheet
Title
Saydnaya: Inside a Syrian Torture Prison
Author(s)
Forensic Architecture (research agency, Goldsmiths, University of London), co-realised with Amnesty International Principal Investigator: Eyal Weizman · Project team: Christina Varvia and others · Acoustic investigation: Lawrence Abu Hamdan
Year
2016 (interactive platform published August 2016; incorporated into Amnesty's "Human Slaughterhouse" report and legal filings through 2017)
Format
Interactive web-based documentary — architectural and acoustic 3D model, witness video testimony, navigable by location or by witness Later republished as a chapter in Eyal Weizman, Forensic Architecture: Violence at the Threshold of Detectability (Zone Books, 2017); exhibited at the ICA London (2016) and elsewhere
Intended Audience
Layered across three registers: the general public, via Amnesty International's advocacy campaign and petition; legal and judicial bodies — the model was submitted to the German Federal Prosecution in a 2017 universal-jurisdiction case; and an academic/design audience, via exhibition, publication, and design-theory discourse.
